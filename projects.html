    CASE STUDY #1

    MedicRegister: Redesigning a Decades-Old Website

    MedicRegister.com is an online platform where individuals who seek medical resources can browse and buy from 45,000 listings of worldwide products and services.

    Users felt overwhelmed and frustrated because all the pages were visually cluttered, had an influx of competing colors, and displayed too much information at once with poor organization. The combination of poor signifiers, misleading tab names, and inconsistent navigation made experiences quite frustrating. Overall, MedicRegister's outdated design caused heavy cognitive overload which slowed them down made simple tasks tricky to perform.

    For this case study, I only adressed and redesigned MedicRegister's home page.

    Wireframe Software: Balsamiq

    What I Took Out:

    The visual noise which made experiences frustrating was corrected through the elimination of excessive colors, cutting back on volume of on-screen content, and removal of ambiguous and inaccurate page names that worked against user comprehension.

    What I Introduced:

    I adopted a clearer structural format that takes after modern interfaces to give users a sense of familiarity in navigation. I also implemented an always-visable top and bottom navigation bar so users can now understand where they are and access other pages from any page. Additionally, I grouped miscellaneous information into more logical categories with consistent distancing. This successfully separated the once dense mixtures of text that made MedicRegister somewhat untrustwory, bringing a sense of clean professionalism to the site.

    Furthermore, collapsible tabs and subtabs bring visual hierarchy to the browser and reduce the amount of clutter on the screen. Lastly, I renamed misleading tab titles to something that accurately describes the content, so users instantly understand each page's purpose with correctness. These kinds of changes alleviate much mental fatigure that came from daunting processes of cutting through information piles.
